Obsidian Reading Surface Atlas

This demo is now treated as a specimen book, not a dashboard. The presentation layer is intentionally more refined, but the inner note remains the calibrated object: real note width, visible properties, title proportion, single-depth code surface, and restrained table contrast are kept aligned to the live Obsidian pane.

Calibration

Designed shell, faithful specimen

  • Judge fidelity by the note inside the stage, not by the surrounding editorial shell.
  • Each option keeps the same vellum material grammar, but shifts its typography rhythm and title behavior.
  • The outer page is allowed to be elegant, but it should never squeeze, crop, or overpower the specimen.

Reading rhythm

In this version of the reading surface, the body text is slightly enlarged, the spacing is airy, and the prose keeps a manuscript-like softness even when discussing branching_time.

Theoretical framework

Information is not merely transmitted; it is arranged. Codex keeps the same vellum shell as the other options, but its typography is more disciplined: regular scale, restrained spacing, and a steadier block texture suited to long sequences of argument, references, and indexable_structure.

The correction here is mostly about measure and hierarchy. In the old three-column demo, Codex felt too editorial because the title and body were forced into a narrow width; at note width, its authority becomes calmer and more documentary.

Editorial cadence

Feuilleton keeps the editorial contrast, but the final version now makes the CJK body more intentional. The article title still enters in Didot, while the body keeps Georgia for Latin text and routes CJK to HYCuFangSongJ, using a larger body scale and tighter tracking to let the fangsong texture show up without sacrificing comfort.

Once the note regains a realistic width, the option reads less like a poster and more like a magazine column. That is closer to the live Obsidian effect visible in the actual pane: display drama at the top, then restraint, with the bilingual body no longer collapsing back into a generic Song texture.
